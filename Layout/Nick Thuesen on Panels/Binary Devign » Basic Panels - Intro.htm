Basic Panels - Intro

DISCLAIMER:
I’m not kidding anyone. If you’re the type of person who likes to read dull, no-nonsense, intense vocabulary filled documents, like I do, then you need to check out the SDK. What I’m trying to do is just give my own prospective in a manner people, who don’t have the time to wade through the SDK, can utilize.
END DISCLAIMER

Here’s the definition the SDK gives for panels. "Panels are components that control the rendering of elements—their size and dimensions, their position, and the arrangement of their child content. Rendering is controlled by properties specified on Panels." There are days when I wish I could talk like that. The thought of nobody understanding me but believing I’m really really smart is totally appealing. Here’s my definition of panels: Panels are like boxes. If you think of your UI (buttons, textboxes etc) as clutter, panels are the boxes you use to keep everything organized. You use different types of boxes to hold and organize your different types of clutter." Sometimes I look at my apartment and wish I had could drop a couple of DropPanels and Stackpanels to it. My disgusting living habits aside, I’m going to discuss the four panels I see and use the most. They are the Grid, DockPanel, StackPanel, and Canvas. Now by no means should you consider these the only panels worth using. The SDK mentions several others and the beauty of panels, like almost everything else in Avalon, is you can make your own. (Fil Fortes has implemented a few of his own panels that are fantastic! Since they’re his babies I’ll let you bug him for a post. They’re worth it!)
