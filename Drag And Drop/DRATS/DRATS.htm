Drag and Resize Alpha-Transparent Shapes


A clock as sample content.

Current with May 2006 WinFX CTP.

I've come up with another method for dragging and resizing alpha-transparent, non-rectangular shapes on the desktop with WPF. It has advantages over my first method in that it doesn't require complete opacity at any point, and that just about anything can be used as content.

If you are WPF enabled you can point Internet Explorer to this XBAP implementation for a quick demonstration. But it won't give the full effect of having the graphic on your desktop (which is kind of the point).

This technique still uses the HwndSource and UsesPerPixelOpacity, but makes the transparent window the size of the entire workspace and then allows the user to drag and resize a scene inside of it.

I hear on the newsgroups that more support is coming stardard in WPF for these types of apps, and also support for controlling z-order of elements. Also, see Lester's Blog! for some info on these things. I just saw some of those entries as I created this page. I need to organize my RSS feeds from all these guys.

Anyway, this app here works pretty well, I think. It has potential to be streamlined into a quick and easy method for getting small scenes on the screen easily.
