Kevin Moore’s WPF DatePicker (Part 1)

Posted by Matthias Shapiro under WPF Sample , datepicker , kevin moore
 

One of the things that drives me crazy in Blend is the fact that there is still no date picker. In my work, the developers I’m working with loved having the old date picker and it makes putting together mid-fidelity prototypes much easier.

Before:

Me: Here’s how we’re doing our search layout… (I show them the layout)
Them: And how do I choose a range of dates?
Me: ‘click’
Them: Oh. Looks good. Here’s a raise.

Now:

Me: Here’s how we’re doing our search layout… (I show them the layout)
Them: And how do I choose a range of dates?
Me: Well, you’ll be able to do that with this button… and just imagine that a calandar comes up and all your wildest date-picking dreams are fulfilled.
Them: Oh. I’m not sure that will work.

Fortunately, we’ve been able to use Kevin Moore’s WPF date picker in his Bag-O-Tricks. We used this in one of our demos and it worked pretty well.

“OK, so how do I use this thing?” you may ask.

Adding Kevin Moore’s Date Picker

1. Download and unzip Kevin’s WPF Samples file.
2. Open up a new Blend Project.
3. Go to the “Project” tab in the upper right hand corner and right click on the “References” folder to add a reference and choose “WPFSamplesLib.dll” from the “WPFSamples/Library/bin/Debug/” folder.
4. Click on the “Asset Library” (the bottom icon in the left toolbar) and click on the “Custom Controls” tab.
5. Choose either the DatePicker control or the MonthCalandarControl and just draw it into the design view and you should see something like what you see below.
