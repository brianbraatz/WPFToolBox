New Video Tutorial on CLR Databinding

January 19th 2007 Posted to C# Code, XAML, Tutorials

I just uploaded a new tutorial to contentpresenter.com that shows how to create a simple collection of your computer’s running processes. After creating the collection in the code-behind I show how you can instantiate it and bind to it in the XAML.

    I think using the clr-namespace and instantiating the object the way you’re showing is fine for certain cases as it’s much simpler xaml markup.

    However, there are several benefits to using the ObjectDataProvider . Beatrix Costa from the WPF team explained it really well at: http://www.beacosta.com/2006/03/why-should-i-use-objectdataprovider.html.

    For example in your sample, we could add a xmlns reference to System.Diagnostics directly and then use an ObjectDataProvider to call the GetProcesses Method, skipping the writing the C# code.
